SPONSOR YOUR SPOUSE OR COMMON LAW PARTNER

Government of Canada strongly supports family reunification and given high priority in processing of these applications to make sure eligible couple live together without any restrictions.

Individuals coming to Canada as students, temporary foreign workers or permanent residents can bring their dependent spouse to Canada for the duration of their stay.

If you are Canadian citizen or permanent resident you have the right to sponsor your spouse, or common law partner to come to Canada if you are able to support them financially and make sure they don’t need social assistance from the government of Canada under family sponsorship program.

Eligibility requirements

To sponsor your spouse or common law partner you must meet the following requirements

  1. at least 18 years old
  2. Canadian citizen, a permanent resident of Canada, or a person registered in Canada as an Indian under the Canadian Indian Act
  3. able to prove that you’re not receiving social assistance for reasons other than a disability
  4. provide for the basic needs of your spouse you want to sponsor
  5. promise an undertaking of financial support and repay any social assistance received

You are ineligible to sponsor if

  • you won’t live in Canada when your spouse become permanent resident
  • you’re a temporary resident, that is you’re visiting, studying or working in Canada on a visa or permit
  • your permanent residence application is still in process
  • were sponsored by a spouse or partner and you became a permanent resident less than 5 years ago
  • are still financially responsible for a previous spouse or partner that you sponsored. This means you’re still bound by the 3-year undertaking to take care of this person.
  • can’t legally stay in Canada and must leave the country because you received a removal order
  • receive social assistance for a reason other than a disability and many other reasons that make you ineligible to sponsor

Government of Canada recognizes same-sex marriage and same-sex partners and you may be eligible if this applies to you.

Apart from the above, the sponsored spouse should also meet some requirements and is not inadmissible to Canada. They should complete medical exams and biometrics as requested by the immigration officer.
